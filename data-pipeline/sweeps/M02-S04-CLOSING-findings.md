# M02-S04 — Closing findings

**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Branch:** `m02/asymmetric-ortony-scoring`
**Status:** Eval-harness retro complete. M02 algorithmic hypothesis empirically refuted on a fair cohort. Programme pivots to M03.

---

## Headline

> The cohort-shape mismatch that S04-A and S04-B identified has been
> resolved by the Haiku+sensorimotor rebuild. With the confound removed,
> **no scoring formula in the pointwise-property-overlap family beats
> the random_uniform null reference**. M02 — Asymmetric Ortony Scoring's
> central hypothesis (asymmetric formulas would meaningfully outperform
> symmetric) is empirically not supported.

What M02 *did* deliver, on reflection, is not the algorithm change it
set out to deliver — it's a **measurement instrument** plus a thorough
inventory of which kinds of changes do *not* move the metric. The
retro work to validate the instrument was substantial; the verdict
is now trustworthy.

## What the result means

### What we falsified

**M02's algorithmic premise.** That asymmetric Ortony-style scoring
(`ortony_vehicle_salience`, `ortony_imbalance`, `ortony_log_ratio`)
would discriminate apt metaphor pairs from MUNCH paraphrase pairs
better than symmetric formulas (`jaccard_salience`, `jaccard_raw`,
`cosine_salience`). On a fair cohort, none of the seven scoring
formulas — symmetric, asymmetric, or null — produces a
`separation_score` distinguishable from random uniformly across runs.

The prior v2/v3 sweeps suggested `ortony_imbalance` had a small edge
(+0.0010). That edge was generated by the cohort-shape mismatch
S04-B identified — the apt cohort had ~30% smaller property unions
than the inapt cohort, so any formula that happened to reward the
slimmer side got an artifactual boost. With both cohorts now
properly enriched, the artifact disappears and `ortony_imbalance`
collapses to −0.0005.

### What we validated (the retro's real wins)

1. **The eval harness measures what it says.** Once the cohort is
   balanced, `random_uniform` lands at +0.0068 — within sampling noise
   of the predicted zero. The harness's discrimination signal is
   real and trustworthy. Future scoring experiments can rely on it.

2. **The `physical → sensorimotor` prompt rename was a substantial
   substrate improvement.** It moved sensorimotor properties per
   synset from ~0.8 to ~5.4 on abstract synsets, with no meaningful
   regression on concrete synsets. Even though it didn't deliver M02's
   target metric, it gives every downstream algorithm a richer base
   to work with.

3. **Haiku is competitive for enrichment.** Faster, cheaper, slightly
   more sensorimotor-dense, comparable per-synset quality. Adopted as
   the production enrichment model going forward.

4. **The cohort-shape diagnostic generalises.** S04-A/B's stratification
   methodology (retention by domain, union-size distributions) is now
   a standard part of the eval-harness toolkit — any future milestone
   that runs the harness should verify cohort shape before trusting a
   verdict.

### What remains genuinely unknown

* Whether **pointwise property-overlap** is fundamentally the wrong
  primitive for this metaphor-detection task, or whether *some*
  scoring fn in this family would work given even-richer data.
* Whether **structural primitives** (concreteness gates, domain-
  distance re-rank, type-aligned matching) — M03/M04 territory — can
  produce above-null separation.
* Whether the **MUNCH cohort is the right inapt reference at all**, or
  whether S04-E (synthetic matched inapt cohort) needs to happen as
  a separate validation track.

## Programme implications

### M02 — Asymmetric Ortony Scoring: closes empirically negative

Per the M02 success criterion (≥5% absolute separation_score
improvement over best symmetric baseline), the milestone fails. None
of the asymmetric variants we built deliver. They stay in the
`SCORING_FNS` registry as committed work — useful priors for future
milestones and the test harness already exercises them.

**S03 (wire winner into Go forge): remains parked and now empirically
correct to park.** There is no winner to wire.

### M03 — Cascade Gate-and-Rank: promotes to Next

M03 was queued behind M02. With M02 closing, M03 becomes the immediate
next algorithm milestone. Its premise is *structural* (concreteness
gate, Ortony rank, domain-distance re-rank) rather than pointwise
formula choice. Now that pointwise has hit a ceiling, structural is
the natural place to search.

The M03 implementation has a much better substrate to work against
than M02 did:

* Cohort balance: apt 271 / inapt 978 (vs 232 / 317)
* Haiku+sensorimotor enrichment: 5.4 sensorimotor properties per
  synset (vs 0.8 for the old prompt+model combination)
* Concreteness data: `synset_concreteness` table (73,927 rows, full
  Brysbaert coverage of the lexicon)
* Domain-distance: `property_dimensions` + `synset_centroids` already
  present; FastText vectors normalised
* Trustworthy eval harness: `random_uniform = +0.0068` confirms

### Backlog items surfaced

* **Pipeline Tooling Consolidation & Relevance Audit** *(already in
  Backlog)* — the four code-backfill items + the relevance audit of
  enrich.sh and the m02_s04_*.py one-offs.
* **Pipeline Architectural Review** *(already in Backlog)* — schema
  change management, seed-data lifecycle, enrichment provenance,
  derived-curation rebuild cadence.
* **The Bridge** *(new — promote to Queued)* — dual of the Forge: given
  source and target, return the path through wordspace linking them.
  Different mechanism class than pointwise scoring (graph search rather
  than ranking). Useful both as an explanatory product feature and as
  a tool for generating semi-supervised inapt examples (bad-bridge =
  bad-metaphor signal). See design sketch in conversation 2026-05-16.
