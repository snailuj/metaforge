This is the "heavyweight" of US student competitions. While it officially begins in 7th Grade (NZ Year 8), it is a major goal for many middle schoolers.
- Angle: use Metaforge to help students polish their *Flash Fiction* or *Poetry* entries, focusing on the "Technical Skill" and "Originality" criteria that judges use.

2. **Kids Write 4 Kids (Grades 4–8)**
This contest aligns perfectly with your exact age niche. It is a nationwide creative writing contest where winners actually get their stories published.
- Angle: pitch Metaforge as a way for 4th–8th graders to "level up" their word choice before submitting their manuscripts.
- Timeline: typically accepts entries through March.

3. **Young Novelist Challenge (formerly NaNoWriMo YWP)**
Following the closure of the original NaNoWriMo organization in 2025, a new team launched the Young Novelist Challenge. It focuses on the joy of storytelling and writing fluency.
- Angle: "gamified" approach perfectly complements their focus on "creative play" and making writing accessible to kids.
- Timeline: big push in November (like NaNoWriMo), but they offer resources and smaller challenges year-round.

4. **NCTE Promising Young Writers (Grade 8)**
Run by the National Council of Teachers of English, this specifically targets 8th graders to recognize their writing talent.
- Angle: since this is a high-level academic recognition, your focus on Tier 2 Vocabulary would be a major selling point for teachers preparing their students.
- Timeline: submissions for the 2026 cycle are open through February 15, 2026.
