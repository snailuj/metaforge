## Academia / Free / Open-Source
### Paul Nation, Victoria University Wellington NZ
> VocabularySize.com is a free service designed to assist teachers and researchers in implementing some of the best practice principles derived from the latest research in second language vocabulary acquisition. It is intended to be *a tribute to the many years of research of Paul Nation*, who tirelessly gave his time and ideas to the applied linguistics community. One of his contributions was the Vocabulary Size Test (VST) which was the original inspiration for this website. Paul Nation has recently claimed that he has retired. Nevertheless, he continues to write, research, and supervise post-graduate students at **Victoria University of Wellington in New Zealand** as well as distance-based students throughout the world. He continues to make frequent trips abroad to teach or collaborate with other researchers and shows no sign of slowing down any time soon.

### VocabularySize.com
> One of the biggest challenges in learning a second or foreign language is not necessarily mastering the grammar, as many people assume, but rather accumulating enough words in order to communicate ideas. Because of the importance of words in using a language, vocabulary size has often been found to strongly correlate with other language proficiency measures such as reading and listening comprehension as well as general language proficiency.

### List from vocabularysize.com's FAQ section
This list might be ten years old by now (being an under-maintained, aged website in itself) but could provide a bit of a head-start.

> Here is a list, in no particular order, of some sites we know about:

### English vocabulary size tests

#### [_The Compleat Lextutor_](http://www.lextutor.ca/ "Compleat Lexical Tutor")

This site, created by [Tom Cobb](http://www.lextutor.ca/cv "Tom Cobb Vita 2011"), hosts [many different vocabulary-related tests](http://www.lextutor.ca/tests "TESTS HOME"), including the [_VST_](http://www.lextutor.ca/tests/levels/recognition/1_14k "LEVELS TEST - RECOGNITION (1-14k)"). Cobb has continuously pioneered vocabulary-based web resources on his site and offered them free of charge for many years, for which we are deeply indebted. _The Compleat Lextutor_ also offers a range of other vocabulary–related services which are invaluable to any serious student, researcher, or practitioner of vocabulary-focused language acquisition.

#### [__lognostics_](http://www.lognostics.co.uk/tools "_lognostics ~ free software")

[Paul Meara](http://www.swansea.ac.uk/staff/academic/ArtsHumanities/mearap "Swansea University - Professor Paul Michael Meara") has been developing a wide–ranging suite of vocabulary-based assessment tools for many years which are distributed for free. The majority of the tests are designed as stand-alone programs for the Windows environment including two vocabulary size tests based on the yes/no format. One measures vocabulary sizes up to the [5,000 most frequent word level](http://www.lognostics.co.uk/tools/X_Lex205.zip "X_Lex v2.05: a 5k vocabulary size test.") while the other measures from the [5,000 to 10,000 frequency levels](http://www.lognostics.co.uk/tools/Y_Lex205.zip "Y_Lex v2.05: a 5-10k vocabulary size test."). Only one test, [Lex30](http://www.lognostics.co.uk/tools/Lex30 "LEX30 V3.00b"), is available online and is unique in that it is one of the only tests which measures _productive_ vocabulary size ([Meara & Fitzpatrick, 2000](http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S0346-251X\(99\)00058-5 "Meara, P & Fitzpatrick, T. (2000). Lex30: an improved method of assessing productive vocabulary in an L2. System, 28(1), 19–30.")). __lognostics_ also has an extensive [bibliography of vocabulary-related research](http://www.lognostics.co.uk/varga "Varga ~ bibliographical resources in vocabulary acquisition").

#### [_It’s All in a Word_](http://homepage.ntlworld.com/vivian.c/Words "Words Website by Vivian Cook")

[Vivian Cook](http://homepage.ntlworld.com/vivian.c/Vivian%20Cook.htm "Vivian Cook: SLA researcher")’s extensive work in applied linguistics also covers vocabulary acquisition. As part of a book he published in 2009, he created two vocabulary size tests based on frequencies form the [British National Corpus](http://www.natcorp.ox.ac.uk/ "[bnc] British National Corpus"). The [basic test](http://homepage.ntlworld.com/vivian.c/Words/WordTests/TESTsizeBasic.htm "Basic English word test by Vivian Cook") measures up to the most common 20,000 words and the [advanced test](http://homepage.ntlworld.com/vivian.c/Words/WordTests/TESTsizeAdvanced.htm "Basic English word test by Vivian  Cook") measures beyond 150,000 words. Cooks’ tests seem to use word _types_ as the unit of measure.

#### [Lexxica’s _V-Check_](http://www.wordengine.jp/vflash/levelcheck "V-Check - たったの５分で英単語力測定")

The team at [Lexxica](http://www.wordengine.jp/about "ワードエンジン運営会社 - Lexxica（レキシカ）について"), researchers Charles Browne and Brent Culligan along with their financial backer Guy Cihi, were (probably) the first to really develop a truly interactive, online, usable vocabulary size test. It also has a facility for teachers to collect their students’ scores from a central location. The utility of their service has been an inspiration to _VocabularySize.com_ and we commend their efforts in bringing true technological advances to the realm of vocabulary size measurement. Their website also has [an extensive library](http://www.wordengine.jp/research/main "リサーチ - ワードエンジン") which explains the challenges and techniques for estimating vocabulary size.

#### [Words & Tools’ _Lemma_ test](http://web.archive.org/web/20070701210834/www.wordsandtools.com/index_lemma.htm "Words and Tools - Lemma")

Unfortunately, this test no longer exists on the web, but a working copy can still be found at [_The Internet Archive_](http://www.archive.org/ "Internet Archive: Digital Library of Free Books, Movies, Music & Wayback Machine"). This test, based on the _Collins Cobuild_ corpus, was created by [Boo Hever](http://www.statvoks.no/ecole/boo.htm "NICOLE homepage") and defines knowing a word as the ability to identify synonyms and/or associates. The test used to be available as a software package that would generate a new test each time. Hever was one of the true pioneers of vocabulary size measurement.

#### [Bruce Zhang’s _Vocabulary Size Test_](http://www.insightin.com/test/test.phtml "Vocabulary Test: Estimate Vocabulary Size Online")

Zhang’s test was created in 2002 and has been available online ever since. We’re not sure exactly how the test estimates vocabulary size, but [some general details about the development of the test](http://www.insightin.com/test/about.shtml "About the Vocabulary Size Test") note that measures of word frequency are based on internet search engine results.

#### [_English Vocabulary Tester_](http://www.javacamp.org/misc/VocTester.html "English Vocabulary Tester")

We are not entirely sure who is behind this project, but the test seems to be based on Diack’s 1975 book, [_Test Your Own Wordpower_](http://books.google.com/books?id=uQhMAAAACAAJ "Wordpower: Your Vocabulary and Its Measurement - Hunter Diack - Google Books").

#### [Frank Horace Vizetelly’s vocabulary size test](http://books.google.com/books?id=CCoDAAAAMBAJ&lpg=PA30&pg=PA30#v=onepage&q&f=false "Popular Science - Google Books")

Vizetelly’s test is one of many from the turn of the 20th century when estimating vocabulary sizes was a trendy research topic. Many of these tests were built upon faulty sampling techniques and tended to overestimate.
