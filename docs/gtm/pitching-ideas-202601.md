## LLM-Enriched, but not LLM-Driven
Our data pipeline utilizes LLMs during build-time to enrich our semantic graph, but the runtime experience is purely deterministic. This ensures the tool is fast, cost-effective, and—most importantly—safe for the classroom, as it eliminates the risks of 'hallucination' or inappropriate AI-generated content.

## Talking up the O/S aspect
Metaforge is built on a fully transparent, MIT-licensed architecture. We are committed to an open-source model to ensure that educational tools for children are auditable, community-driven, and free from the 'black box' logic of proprietary software.

### Repo "private for now"
While we are currently in a private pre-release phase to refine our Golang core, we are committed to a public MIT-license launch upon reaching our first stable milestone. This allows us to build a robust foundation for future community contributors.

We are currently developing Metaforge, an MIT-licensed, open-source writing companion for Middle Grades students (Grades 4–8).

Metaforge fills a critical gap in the US education market by providing a Privacy-by-Design tool that gamifies figurative language and vocabulary acquisition. Our architecture is unique: we utilize a headless Golang API to serve a deterministic semantic network. While we use LLMs for build-time data enrichment, the student-facing runtime is entirely AI-free to ensure pedagogical safety and COPPA compliance.
